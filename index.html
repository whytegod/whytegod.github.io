Whytegod Protocol

Whytegod is a minimalist blockchain protocol designed for permanent, verifiable record anchoring without governance, identity, or discretionary control.

Core Principles

  • Fixed supply
  • No governance
  • No identity layer
  • Deterministic verification
  • Long-term auditability

Native Asset

  • Primary unit: WGD
  • Smallest unit: Wert

Records

Whytegod records are immutable anchors designed to persist independently of applications, users, or institutions.

Protocol

The protocol enforces minimalism by limiting surface area, avoiding governance logic, and prioritizing deterministic state validation.

Audit

All protocol rules are transparent, deterministic, and verifiable without reliance on trusted intermediaries.

Threat Model

Whytegod assumes adversarial environments, censorship pressure, and institutional decay, optimizing for survivability and verification over convenience.

Economics

The economic design emphasizes scarcity, predictability, and resistance to manipulation rather than growth incentives or inflationary rewards.
