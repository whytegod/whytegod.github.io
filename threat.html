Threat Model

Security Assumptions and Limitations

Purpose

This document defines the threat model for the Whytegod Protocol. It identifies which risks are addressed by protocol design and which risks are explicitly out of scope.

Assumptions

  • Cryptographic hash functions remain secure
  • The underlying anchoring layer provides immutability
  • Public verification data remains accessible

Threats Addressed

  • Post-publication record modification
  • Timestamp manipulation after confirmation
  • Anchor substitution attacks
  • Unauthorized record mutation

Threats Not Addressed

  • False or malicious data submitted by users
  • Loss or compromise of private keys
  • Identity spoofing or impersonation
  • Legal, regulatory, or jurisdictional disputes

Denial of Service

The protocol does not prevent denial-of-service attacks at the network or infrastructure level. Economic costs associated with anchoring act as a partial deterrent but do not eliminate this risk.

Trust Boundaries

The protocol minimizes trust assumptions. No trust is placed in operators, maintainers, or external authorities beyond cryptographic verification and public anchoring.
