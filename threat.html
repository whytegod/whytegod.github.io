Threat Model

Security Assumptions and Limitations

Design Assumptions

  • Underlying cryptographic primitives remain secure
  • Anchoring layer provides immutability
  • Public verification is available to all participants

Threats Addressed

  • Record tampering after anchoring
  • Timestamp manipulation
  • Anchor replacement attacks
  • Unauthorized record mutation

Threats Not Addressed

  • Identity theft or impersonation
  • Malicious data submitted by users
  • Legal or jurisdictional disputes
  • Loss of private keys by users

Denial of Service

The protocol does not prevent denial-of-service attacks at the network or infrastructure layer. Economic costs associated with anchoring provide a natural deterrent against abuse.

Trust Boundaries

Trust is minimized to cryptographic verification and public anchoring. No trust is placed in operators, maintainers, or external authorities.
