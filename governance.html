Governance

Governance in the Whytegod Protocol is minimal by design. The protocol favors stability, predictability, and neutrality.

Governance Philosophy

  • Structure over discretion
  • Rules over authority
  • Signals over control
  • Forks over force

What Governance Can Change

  • Future protocol versions
  • Optional extensions
  • Fee parameters for new deployments
  • Documentation clarifications

What Governance Cannot Change

  • Previously anchored records
  • Historical hashes
  • Past protocol rules
  • Existing verification logic

Token Holder Signaling

Token holders may signal preferences regarding protocol evolution.

  • Signals are non-binding
  • No automatic execution
  • No custodial power

Proposal Process

  1. Proposal is publicly drafted
  2. Discussion period is opened
  3. Token signaling may occur
  4. Implementation is optional

Forking as Governance

The ultimate governance mechanism is the fork. Anyone may implement a modified protocol version.

Adoption — not permission — determines success.

No Central Authority

  • No admin keys
  • No override functions
  • No emergency controls

Protocol Neutrality

The protocol does not favor:

  • Specific blockchains
  • Specific jurisdictions
  • Specific use cases

Legal Independence

Governance signaling does not constitute ownership, partnership, or fiduciary duty.

Governance exists to guide evolution — not to control the protocol.
