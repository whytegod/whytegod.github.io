Governance Model

Whytegod has no on-chain governance, no voting system, and no authority that can modify protocol rules. This is an intentional design choice.

No Voting

There is no DAO, council, foundation vote, or token-based decision mechanism. Rules are not changed by consensus or majority.

No Control by Token Holders

Holding WGD does not grant:

  • Voting rights
  • Protocol control
  • Rule modification power
  • Ownership or authority

Why Governance Is Removed

  • Governance introduces capture risk
  • Voting enables coercion and bribery
  • Mutable rules undermine permanence
  • Neutral protocols must remain fixed

Frozen Rules

Whytegod v1.0 rules are immutable. Once published, they cannot be altered, paused, or overridden.

How Changes Happen

If changes are ever required, they occur only by publishing a new protocol version. Old versions remain valid forever.

What This Guarantees

  • No retroactive rule changes
  • No censorship
  • No political influence
  • No trust dependency

Who Enforces the Rules

Mathematics, cryptography, and public anchors. Not people. Not organizations. Not Whytegod.

Whytegod defines rules once and steps aside. Users choose whether to follow them.
