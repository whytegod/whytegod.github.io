Economic Design

Native Asset: WGD

Asset Definition

WGD is the native protocol asset used to pay for permanent record anchoring and verification. It is not a governance token and does not grant voting or administrative rights.

Unit Structure

  • WGD — base unit
  • Wert — smallest divisible unit
  • All fees are denominated in Wert

Supply Constraints

The supply of WGD is constrained by protocol issuance rules. No discretionary minting or administrative supply changes are permitted.

Demand-Based Pricing

Demand for WGD arises from the need to create permanent records. As anchoring usage increases, demand pressure may increase the market value of WGD. The protocol itself does not target or enforce a price.

Fee Mechanics

  • Fees are paid at record creation
  • Fees compensate anchoring and verification costs
  • No variable or negotiated pricing
  • Fee schedules are deterministic

Economic Neutrality

The protocol does not subsidize usage, incentivize speculation, or provide yield mechanisms. Economic activity is driven purely by demand for immutable records.
