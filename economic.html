Economic Model

The Whytegod Protocol defines a native unit of value to support long-term record anchoring and verification. This model is intentionally minimal.

Native Asset

  • Name: WGD
  • Smallest Unit: Wert
  • Purpose: Cost signaling, usage accounting, future fee abstraction

Why a Native Asset?

  • To prevent spam and abuse
  • To reflect real anchoring costs
  • To remain neutral across blockchains
  • To avoid centralized payment processors

Current State

At present, WGD is not required to use the protocol. Users anchor records directly on public systems of their choice.

WGD exists as a defined unit for future interoperability and accounting — not as a promise of value.

Potential Future Uses

  • Standardized anchoring fees
  • Cross-chain anchoring abstraction
  • Public record indexing incentives
  • Proof-of-anchoring receipts

What WGD Is Not

  • Not a security
  • Not a governance token
  • Not a yield instrument
  • Not required for verification

Economic Sustainability

The protocol is designed to remain valid even if WGD is never widely adopted.

Records remain verifiable as long as their anchors exist.

Whytegod defines economic structure — not financial guarantees.
