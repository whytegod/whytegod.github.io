Economic Model

Native Asset: WGD

Purpose of WGD

WGD is the native protocol asset used to pay for record anchoring and verification within the Whytegod Protocol. It exists solely to allocate scarce anchoring resources and does not grant governance, ownership, or administrative privileges.

Unit Structure

  • WGD — primary unit
  • Wert — smallest divisible unit
  • All protocol fees are denominated in Wert

Supply Discipline

The supply of WGD is constrained by protocol-defined issuance rules. No discretionary minting, rebasing, or administrative supply changes are permitted once the protocol is deployed.

Demand-Based Dynamics

Demand for WGD is driven exclusively by usage of the protocol. As more records are anchored, demand for WGD may increase. The protocol itself does not target, stabilize, or guarantee any market price.

Fee Mechanics

  • Fees are paid at the time of record anchoring
  • Fees are deterministic and non-negotiable
  • No discounts, subsidies, or incentive programs exist
  • Fee schedules are publicly verifiable

Economic Neutrality

The protocol does not provide yield, rewards, or speculative incentives. Economic activity emerges solely from demand for permanent, verifiable records.
