Verify a Record

Verification proves that a record existed unchanged at a specific point in time. No trust in Whytegod is required.

What You Need

  • The original data
  • The recorded WRID hash
  • The public anchor reference

Verification Process

  1. Recompute the SHA-256 hash from the original data
  2. Compare it to the claimed WRID
  3. Confirm the WRID appears in the public anchor
  4. Confirm the anchor timestamp
Whytegod Verification Result

Recomputed Hash: MATCH
WRID: CONFIRMED
Anchor: FOUND
Timestamp: VERIFIED

Status: VALID RECORD

If the hash does not match, the record is invalid.

What Verification Proves

  • The data has not changed
  • The record existed before the anchor time
  • No authority approval was required

What It Does NOT Prove

  • Ownership
  • Identity
  • Truth or intent

Verification is permanent, free, and permissionless. Anyone can verify — forever.
