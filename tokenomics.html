Tokenomics

The Whytegod Protocol token is a utility coordination asset. It does not represent equity, ownership, or profit rights.

Purpose of the Token

  • Prevent spam and ledger abuse
  • Align long-term network participation
  • Support sustainable protocol operations
  • Enable governance signaling

What the Token Is Not

  • Not a share
  • Not a security
  • Not a promise of profit
  • Not required for verification

Supply

The token supply is fixed. No minting function exists after genesis.

  • Total supply: Defined at launch
  • Inflation: None
  • Burn mechanism: Protocol-defined

Distribution

  • Protocol development
  • Infrastructure and maintenance
  • Community incentives
  • Public access mechanisms

Distribution is transparent and publicly auditable.

Usage Within the Protocol

  • Optional anchoring priority
  • Rate-limiting protection
  • Governance proposal signaling

Anchoring Fees

Anchoring data may require a token fee. Fees exist to prevent abuse — not to restrict access.

  • Verification remains free
  • Fees are deterministic
  • No hidden costs

Burn Logic

A portion of anchoring fees may be burned. Burning reduces circulating supply over time.

Burn rules are defined at the protocol level and cannot be changed retroactively.

Governance Role

Token holders may signal preferences on protocol evolution.

  • No automatic control
  • No unilateral authority
  • Signals are advisory

Economic Neutrality

The protocol is designed to remain economically neutral. Its primary function is data anchoring, not speculation.

Risk Disclosure

  • Token value may fluctuate
  • Utility may change with adoption
  • No guarantees are provided

Tokenomics exist to support the protocol — not to define its purpose.
