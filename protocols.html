Protocol Rules

Anchoring, Lifecycle, and Enforcement

Protocol Scope

The Whytegod Protocol defines deterministic rules for anchoring records, validating commitments, and enforcing immutability. The protocol does not manage identity, governance, or arbitration.

Anchoring Rules

  • All records are anchored through cryptographic commitments
  • Anchors reference an external immutable layer
  • Anchor references must be verifiable by any third party
  • No anchor replacement or re-anchoring is permitted
  • Anchors are final once accepted

Lifecycle Rules

  • Record creation is permissionless
  • Validation occurs at anchor time
  • No update, revoke, or delete operations exist
  • Records persist indefinitely
  • Protocol state grows monotonically

Conflict Resolution

Conflicts are resolved deterministically using record hash ordering and anchor timestamp precedence. No human or governance intervention is allowed.

Economic Interaction (WGD)

The native asset WGD is used exclusively for protocol-level interactions such as anchoring and verification. Pricing pressure emerges from demand for permanent records rather than speculative incentives.

Protocol Guarantees

  • Immutability by design
  • Public verifiability
  • Neutral, non-discretionary execution
  • No dependency on off-chain trust
