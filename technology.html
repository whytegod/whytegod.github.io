Protocol Research: Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange protocol that allows users to trade tokens directly from their wallets without relying on order books or centralized intermediaries.

Core Mechanism

Uniswap uses an Automated Market Maker (AMM) model. Instead of matching buyers and sellers, it relies on liquidity pools funded by users. Prices are determined algorithmically using a constant product formula: 

x * y = k

Where x and y represent the quantities of two tokens in a pool, and k remains constant after each trade.

Liquidity Providers

Anyone can become a liquidity provider by depositing token pairs into a pool. In return, liquidity providers earn a portion of the trading fees proportional to their share of the pool.

This model removes the need for traditional market makers but introduces new risks, including impermanent loss.

Permissionless Access

Uniswap does not require accounts, approvals, or custody. Any compatible wallet can interact with the protocol directly.

This design improves accessibility but shifts responsibility entirely to the user.

Limitations and Risks

  • Price slippage during large trades
  • Impermanent loss for liquidity providers
  • Smart contract risk
  • Network congestion and gas costs

These trade-offs are structural, not bugs. Understanding them is critical to using decentralized systems responsibly.

Why Uniswap Matters

Uniswap demonstrates that open, non-custodial financial infrastructure can function at global scale without centralized control.
