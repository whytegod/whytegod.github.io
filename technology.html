Technology & Protocol Research

Independent, neutral research into decentralized financial systems.

Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange protocol that allows users to trade digital assets directly from their wallets without centralized intermediaries.

Automated Market Maker (AMM)

Instead of order books, Uniswap uses liquidity pools where prices are determined algorithmically.

Liquidity Providers

Users can supply token pairs to pools and earn trading fees proportional to their contribution.

Core Risks

  • Impermanent loss
  • Smart contract risk
  • Price volatility

Aave

Aave is a decentralized lending protocol that enables users to lend and borrow digital assets without traditional banks.

Lending & Borrowing

Depositors earn interest, while borrowers provide collateral to access liquidity.

Risk Management

Loans are overcollateralized and liquidated automatically if safety thresholds are breached.

Core Risks

  • Liquidation risk
  • Oracle dependency
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities

MakerDAO

MakerDAO is a decentralized protocol that issues DAI, a crypto-collateralized stablecoin governed on-chain.

DAI Stablecoin

DAI is generated when users lock collateral and mint debt against it.

Governance

MKR holders vote on system parameters, collateral types, and risk controls.

Core Risks

  • De-pegging risk
  • Governance capture
  • Systemic liquidation events

WHYTEGOD Core Identity

WHYTEGOD is not a token, protocol, or financial product. It is a research-driven initiative focused on understanding decentralized financial coordination.

The project studies how markets, credit, and money behave when governed by code instead of institutions.

  • Markets — decentralized exchange systems
  • Credit — lending and leverage mechanisms
  • Money — issuance and stability models

Risk & Ethics

Decentralized finance involves significant risk, including the possibility of total loss.

WHYTEGOD does not provide financial advice, does not custody assets, and does not influence transactions.

Ethical Position

  • Education over speculation
  • Transparency over persuasion
  • User sovereignty over control

All content is educational and observational. Users are responsible for their own decisions and regulatory compliance.
