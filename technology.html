Protocol Research: Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange protocol that enables peer-to-peer token trading without centralized intermediaries or traditional order books.

Automated Market Maker (AMM)

Uniswap replaces order books with liquidity pools. Prices are determined algorithmically based on token ratios.

Liquidity Provision

Users supply token pairs to pools and earn fees proportional to their share of liquidity.

Constant Product Formula

The protocol maintains the invariant x × y = k, ensuring continuous liquidity under changing market conditions.

Risks

  • Impermanent loss
  • Smart contract risk
  • Price volatility

WHYTEGOD studies Uniswap as a foundational coordination mechanism for decentralized markets.

Protocol Research: Aave

Aave is a decentralized liquidity protocol that enables lending and borrowing of digital assets without intermediaries.

Lending Pools

Users deposit assets into pools, making them available for borrowing by others who provide collateral.

Interest Rate Model

Interest rates adjust automatically based on utilization, balancing liquidity across the system.

Overcollateralization

Borrowers must provide collateral exceeding the value of the loan to reduce default risk.

Liquidation Mechanism

If collateral value drops below safety thresholds, positions are liquidated automatically.

Flash Loans

Aave introduced flash loans — uncollateralized loans that must be repaid within a single transaction.

Risks

  • Oracle dependency
  • Liquidation risk
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities

WHYTEGOD studies Aave to understand decentralized credit, leverage, and automated risk management.

Protocol Research: MakerDAO

MakerDAO is a decentralized protocol that issues DAI, a crypto-collateralized stablecoin governed on-chain.

DAI Stablecoin

DAI is generated when users lock collateral into smart contracts and mint debt against it.

Vaults (CDPs)

Vaults enforce minimum collateral ratios to maintain system stability.

Stability Mechanisms

Stability fees, liquidation penalties, and governance controls are used to maintain DAI’s peg.

Governance

MKR token holders vote on risk parameters, collateral types, and protocol upgrades.

Risks

  • De-pegging risk
  • Governance capture
  • Oracle failures
  • Liquidation cascades

WHYTEGOD studies MakerDAO as an experiment in decentralized money and monetary policy.

WHYTEGOD: Core Philosophy

WHYTEGOD is not a protocol, token, or financial product. It is a research-driven coordination framework focused on understanding decentralized financial systems at their foundation.

The project exists to observe how markets, credit, and money behave when coordination is handled by code rather than institutions.

Foundational Focus

  • Markets — decentralized exchange (Uniswap)
  • Credit — lending and leverage (Aave)
  • Money — issuance and stability (MakerDAO)

Neutrality & Non-Custodial Design

WHYTEGOD does not custody assets, execute transactions, or influence financial decisions.

The project prioritizes clarity, transparency, and analytical neutrality.

Human Behavior & Risk

Although decentralized finance is automated, outcomes are shaped by human incentives, governance decisions, and behavioral stress.

WHYTEGOD focuses on where human behavior intersects with automated systems.

Long-Term Objective

The long-term goal is to develop open research frameworks that help individuals, DAOs, and institutions reason clearly about decentralized financial systems without surrendering sovereignty.
