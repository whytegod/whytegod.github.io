Protocol Research: Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange (DEX) protocol that enables peer-to-peer trading of digital assets without order books or intermediaries.

Automated Market Maker (AMM)

Instead of matching buyers and sellers, Uniswap uses liquidity pools. Prices are determined by a mathematical formula based on asset ratios.

Liquidity Provision

Users supply token pairs into pools and earn fees generated from trades. Liquidity providers assume risk in exchange for yield.

Constant Product Formula

Uniswap maintains the invariant x * y = k, ensuring liquidity is always available at algorithmically adjusted prices.

Risks

  • Impermanent loss
  • Smart contract risk
  • Oracle-free price exposure

WHYTEGOD studies Uniswap as a foundational example of permissionless market infrastructure.

Protocol Research: Aave

Aave is a decentralized liquidity protocol that enables users to lend and borrow digital assets without intermediaries.

Core Function

Users deposit assets into liquidity pools. These assets become available for borrowing by other users who provide collateral.

Interest Rate Model

Interest rates adjust algorithmically based on utilization. Higher demand increases borrowing costs and balances liquidity.

Overcollateralization

Borrowers must lock collateral worth more than the loan value, reducing default risk.

Liquidation System

Positions are automatically liquidated if collateral value falls below safety thresholds.

Flash Loans

Aave pioneered flash loans — loans that must be borrowed and repaid within a single transaction block.

Risks

  • Smart contract vulnerabilities
  • Oracle dependency
  • Liquidation risk
  • Governance risk

WHYTEGOD studies Aave to understand decentralized credit, risk management, and liquidity coordination.
