Technology & Protocol Research

Protocol Research: Uniswap

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange protocol that enables peer-to-peer token trading without order books or centralized intermediaries.

Automated Market Maker (AMM)

Uniswap replaces traditional order books with liquidity pools. Prices are determined algorithmically based on token ratios.

Liquidity Provision

Users supply token pairs to pools and earn trading fees proportional to their share of liquidity.

Constant Product Formula

The protocol maintains the invariant x × y = k, ensuring continuous liquidity.

Risks

  • Impermanent loss
  • Smart contract risk
  • Price volatility

WHYTEGOD studies Uniswap as a foundational example of permissionless market coordination.

Protocol Research: Aave

Aave is a decentralized liquidity protocol enabling users to lend and borrow digital assets without intermediaries.

Lending Pools

Users deposit assets into pools which become available for borrowing. Borrowers provide collateral to secure loans.

Interest Rate Model

Interest rates adjust automatically based on supply and demand, balancing liquidity across the system.

Overcollateralization

Loans require collateral worth more than the borrowed amount, protecting the protocol against default.

Liquidation Mechanism

If collateral value falls below safety thresholds, positions are liquidated automatically.

Flash Loans

Aave introduced flash loans — uncollateralized loans that must be repaid within a single transaction.

Risks

  • Oracle dependency
  • Liquidation risk
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities

WHYTEGOD studies Aave to understand decentralized credit and algorithmic risk management.

Protocol Research: MakerDAO

MakerDAO is a decentralized protocol that issues DAI, a crypto-collateralized stablecoin.

DAI Stablecoin

DAI is generated when users lock collateral into smart contracts and mint debt against it.

Vaults (CDPs)

Collateralized vaults allow users to create DAI while enforcing minimum collateral ratios.

Stability Mechanisms

Stability fees, liquidation penalties, and governance controls help maintain DAI’s price stability.

Governance

MKR token holders vote on risk parameters, collateral types, and protocol upgrades.

Risks

  • De-pegging risk
  • Governance capture
  • Oracle failures
  • Liquidation cascades

WHYTEGOD studies MakerDAO as an experiment in decentralized money and on-chain monetary policy.
