Audit & Verification

What Can Be Verified and How

Audit Philosophy

The Whytegod Protocol is designed for independent verification. No trust in maintainers or operators is required to validate records, anchoring, or economic rules.

Audit Scope

  • Record structure and immutability
  • Anchoring rules and timestamps
  • Lifecycle state transitions
  • Economic constraints related to WGD

Out of Scope

  • User-generated data correctness
  • External service availability
  • Regulatory or legal compliance
  • Market price fluctuations

Verification Process

  1. Retrieve public record data
  2. Verify cryptographic hashes
  3. Confirm anchor timestamps
  4. Validate lifecycle transitions

Reproducibility

All protocol rules are deterministic. Independent auditors can reproduce verification results using public data and open specifications without privileged access.
