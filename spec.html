Protocol Specification

1. Native Unit

The native unit of the Whytegod protocol is WHY. The smallest indivisible unit is called Wert.

1 WHY = 10⁸ Wert

2. Supply

The total supply of WHY is fixed and immutable. No additional units can be created after genesis. Supply changes are mathematically impossible.

3. Issuance Model

Issuance follows a deterministic, height-based schedule enforced at the header level. Nodes independently verify issuance without relying on trust or governance.

4. Consensus Assumptions

The protocol assumes partial synchrony and adversarial network conditions. No identity, reputation, or permission model exists at the protocol layer.

5. Invariants

  • Supply cannot inflate
  • Records cannot be altered once anchored
  • History cannot be rewritten past finality
  • Privacy is preserved by design, not policy

6. Non-Goals

Whytegod explicitly avoids smart contracts, governance voting, identity systems, and speculative complexity. Utility and permanence are prioritized.
