Records Specification

Immutable Data Anchoring

Definition

A record is a permanent data commitment anchored through the Whytegod Protocol. Once created, a record is immutable and globally verifiable.

Record Format

  • Record ID — deterministic hash identifier
  • Timestamp — time of anchoring
  • Payload Hash — content commitment
  • Anchor Reference — chain or layer reference
  • Checksum — integrity validation

Immutability Rules

  • Records cannot be edited or deleted
  • Conflicts resolve deterministically
  • No overwrite or rollback mechanism
  • All records are final

Verification

Any third party can verify a record by recomputing its hash and validating its anchor reference against the protocol rules.
