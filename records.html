Protocol Records

Transparent system-level history without exposing individual users.

Purpose of Records

Whytegod Records exist to preserve long-term trust while respecting privacy. Individual transactions remain confidential, while protocol-level changes are publicly documented.

This separation prevents governance abuse, silent upgrades, and historical ambiguity.

Record Classification

Only events that alter protocol behavior are eligible for permanent records.

Tier 1 — Structural Records

Consensus rules, emission schedule, cryptographic primitives.

Tier 2 — Operational Records

Network parameters, validator thresholds, protocol performance tuning.

Tier 3 — Informational Records

Documentation changes and non-consensus metadata.

Record Integrity

Records are immutable once published. No deletions, no silent edits. Corrections require a new record referencing the original.

This guarantees historical clarity while maintaining protocol neutrality.
